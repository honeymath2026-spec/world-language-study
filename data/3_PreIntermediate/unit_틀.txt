            <h2>Core Reading: Human Connections</h2>
            <div class="reading-box">
                <p><strong>Part 1: The Social Circle</strong></p>
                <p id="text-origin">Relationships are the foundation of our daily lives. Most people <strong>have</strong> a close-knit group of family and friends. Take Maya and Laura, for instance; they <strong>are</strong> colleagues who often <strong>meet</strong> for coffee. Maya <strong>is currently waiting</strong> for her friend Anna, an exchange student from Poland who <strong>is studying</strong> here.</p>
                
                <div>
                    <p><strong>Part 2: Nature vs. Nurture</strong></p>
                    <p>But what defines our character? Scientists often debate whether it is our genes or our childhood. Some argue that our biology—the DNA we <strong>inherit</strong> from our parents—determines if we are 
                        <strong><span class="vocab-link" onclick="centralizeScroll('vocab-patient')">patient</span></strong>, 
                        <strong><span class="vocab-link" onclick="centralizeScroll('vocab-strict')">strict</span></strong>, or 
                        <strong><span class="vocab-link" onclick="centralizeScroll('vocab-bright')">bright</span></strong>. 
                        Others believe that our environment, such as how our parents <strong>treated</strong> us and who our friends <strong>were</strong>, <strong>forms</strong> our personality.
                    </p>
                </div>

                <p><strong>Part 3: The Art of Communication</strong></p>
                <p>To maintain these bonds, we must communicate. Humans <strong>are</strong> the great communicators; we <strong>have developed</strong> about 6,000 languages to exchange complex ideas. However, we aren't alone. Animals exchange information too—bees <strong>dance</strong> to show where food is, elephants <strong>use</strong> low sounds, and whales <strong>sing</strong>. Yet, human language remains unique in its ability to discuss the past, present, and <strong>future intentions</strong>.</p>
            </div>
